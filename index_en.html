Who am I

Expert in media and communication, with strong experience as a communication officer in the context of international organizations, diplomatic representation, and communication strategy development. I covered events in Syria for numerous local and international media outlets since the beginning of the Syrian revolution. I left Syria in 2017 after our home was bombed and we were displaced from the city of Aleppo in late 2016.

When I arrived in France at the end of 2018, I was invited to speak about Syria at several festivals, high schools, and universities in France, in order to share my expertise, my work, and explain the situation in Syria. I covered many important events, including visits of several presidents to the Élysée Palace. I also covered on numerous conferences and events at UNESCO.

Alongside my studies in Information and communication at Sorbonne University, I worked as a Community Manager for the Sorbonne United Nations Association (SONU) from 2023 to 2024. In 2024, I was selected by Olympic Broadcasting Services (OBS) to join their production department for the Paris 2024 Olympic Games as a production assistant and in the press office.

At the end of my undergraduate studies, I worked and trained as a Communication Officer and Community Manager at UNESCO.

I graduated from Sorbonne University in 2025 with a bachelor’s degree in Information and communication, awarded with Mention Bien (equivalent to high honors in the United States and an honours degree in the United Kingdom).

Outside of my studies and career, I combine my interests by creating digital and social media related content on various platforms.
